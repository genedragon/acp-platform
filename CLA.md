# Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

**ACP — Agentic Collaboration Platform**

Thank you for contributing to ACP. This Contributor License Agreement ("Agreement") documents the rights granted by contributors to the project maintainers. This is a legally binding document.

---

## Why We Need a CLA

ACP is currently licensed under the Business Source License 1.1 (BSL 1.1), which converts to Apache 2.0 after four years. A CLA ensures the project maintainers have the flexibility to:

- Re-license contributions if the project's license evolves
- Offer commercial licenses to partners and enterprises
- Protect contributors and users from patent claims

Without a CLA, every contributor's code is locked to the license at time of submission, making future license changes legally complex.

## How to Sign

**For individual contributors:** By opening a Pull Request and including the text below in the PR description (or as a comment), you indicate your agreement to this CLA:

```
I have read the CLA and agree to its terms.
```

**For corporate contributors:** Please contact the maintainers to execute a Corporate CLA before submitting contributions on behalf of your organization.
